Matchday 2 — What we learned

Spain is fine. Yamal made sure.

After a 0-0 draw with Cabo Verde (yes, really), Yamal scored 10 minutes into his first start vs Saudi Arabia — becoming the 8th youngest scorer in World Cup history. Spain won 4-0. Stamatis, Saad, Gerasimos, María Camila, Dani B., and Alyson — your champion is back.

Colombia is through 🇨🇴

Daniel Muñoz scored in the 76th minute to beat DR Congo 1-0. James Rodríguez created 5 goal-scoring chances — matching Carlos Valderrama's legendary 1998 record. Colombia top Group K with 6 points. Isabel and María Camila, your pick is officially in the Round of 32.

Messi is being unfair.

Hat-trick vs Algeria. Double vs Austria. 5 goals in 2 games at 38 years old. He just became the all-time leading scorer in World Cup history. The only IR pick on Messi as top scorer? Dan Tovar. Of course.

France looked sharp.

Mbappé scored twice vs Senegal, twice vs Iraq. 4 goals, 2 games. Zeshan, Isabel, and Criz picked France — they're feeling good. 8 people picked Mbappé as top scorer. He's right there chasing Messi.

Kane is back. Loudly.

England beat Croatia 4-2. Kane scored twice — a retaken penalty and a header — reaching 10 World Cup goals in his career, tying Gary Lineker as England's all-time top scorer at a World Cup. Isabel and María Camila, your top scorer pick just showed up.

Portugal: mixed signals.

Dan and Cesar both picked Portugal to win it all. First game? A 1-1 draw with DR Congo. Second game? A 5-0 demolition of Uzbekistan. Ronaldo scored and broke yet another record. Portugal is either very good or very inconsistent. We'll find out in Matchday 3.
